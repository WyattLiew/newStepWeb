Future Explorations

In view towards post-corona world

A shift to focus on hygiene

Being scrupulously clean is now the goal as consumers have been educated that handwashing is the key to fight the virus.

With these thoughts in mind, how do we effortlessly calm the nerves of consumers who may become doubtful about trying on products specially to visualize colors on their own skin without compromising hygiene .

We believe high levels of hygiene will be visibly documented on packaging and messages . Make -up applicators and brushes will come with high antimicrobial resistance . Testers will offer innovative ways of trying product with individual capsules and ampoules to ensure safety .

Virtual try -on will be accelerated, moving from experiential to vital for beauty brands . Hands -free application of products such as serums, cleansers, moisturizers, airless pumps, spatulas and smart plastic that resist bacteria and viruses will grow in popularity .

Virtual Try-on

This ultimate non -touch try -on was previously considered as a quirky nice to have ; a tool that engaged consumers, especially the Generation Z .

However, the outbreak has encouraged consumers to virtually try on products and getting used to this tech . This behavior will stick beyond coronavirus .

Consumers were becoming accustomed to connecting each other through a screen, filters that apply facial enhancements could become more mainstream

Beauty brands will accelerate their investment in tech that enables consumers to test products with more authentic representation .

As virtual try -on technology advances, it will gain mass appeal and could move to other categories, such as skincare and hairstyling .
